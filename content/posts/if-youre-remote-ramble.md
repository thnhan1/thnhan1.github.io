---
title: "If you're remote, ramble"
date: 2025-07-15T14:30:00Z
draft: false
topics: ["remote work", "communication", "habits"]
---

Working remotely gives us the opportunity to think more deeply about our communication. Without the casual office interactions, we need to be more intentional about how we connect with our colleagues.

One of the best ways to do this is to embrace the ramble - to take the time to explain your thinking process, to share the context behind your decisions, to let people into your mind.

When you're working in person, a lot of communication happens through body language, quick desk chats, and shared context. Remote work removes many of these channels, so we need to compensate by being more explicit in our written communication.

Don't just share your conclusion - share the journey that got you there. The detours, the dead ends, the moments of insight. These are what help your team understand not just what you think, but how you think.
